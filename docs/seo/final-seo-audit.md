# SignalOps Final SEO Launch Audit

Last updated: May 6, 2026

This audit records the final pre-launch QA pass for the SignalOps rebrand and SEO foundation. It covers brand consistency, public-page metadata, alternative-intent pages, sitemap/robots output, internal links, structured data, safety-sensitive copy, and post-deployment checks.

## Rebrand Checks

- SignalOps is the official brand in user-facing site copy, metadata, JSON-LD, docs, logos, footer links, and shared constants.
- The primary offer is consistently positioned as the Free Missed Lead Check.
- The primary tagline is `Every lead answered. Every follow-up handled.`
- Old predecessor-brand references were searched across the repo.
- The only intentional predecessor-brand mentions are on ethical alternative-intent pages and related data files, where SignalOps includes clear non-affiliation language.
- Legacy demo/client references were searched and no active outdated demo branding remains.
- RouteWash Mobile Fleet Care is the featured mobile fleet wash demo client.
- No hardcoded production secrets or API keys were found in source files.

## SEO Checks

- Public pages have unique page titles.
- Public pages have unique meta descriptions.
- Public pages have canonical URLs generated by the shared SEO helper.
- Open Graph metadata exists for social sharing.
- Twitter/X card metadata exists.
- Default and page-specific OG image assets exist under `/public/og`.
- Public pages have one clear H1.
- Section heading hierarchy is logical and readable.
- Important page copy is server-rendered and crawlable.
- Sitemap generation works and includes intentional public SEO pages.
- Robots generation works and disallows private/internal/API routes.
- Dashboard remains treated as a demo/internal-style route rather than a primary indexable SEO page.
- Internal links in the main nav and grouped footer point to valid routes.
- CTAs point to `/audit`.
- FAQ schema is generated from visible FAQ content on pages that include FAQ sections.
- No fake review, rating, or aggregate-rating schema was found.
- Copy avoids keyword stuffing and thin duplicate page patterns.

## Alternative Page Checks

- Alternative pages use ethical comparison language and avoid impersonation.
- Each alternative page states that SignalOps is not affiliated with the named brand.
- The pages avoid unsupported claims about other products.
- Each page has a distinct angle, including lead response, intake, routing, scoring, inbound handling, or quote intake.
- Each page links to `/audit`, `/demo`, `/how-it-works`, and `/roi-calculator`.
- The alternatives hub links to all alternative-intent pages.

## Safety And Compliance Checks

- Fleet wash copy uses site-requirement-first language for access, timing, water, parking, and local operating constraints.
- The site does not promise compliance, runoff handling, or final service setup before requirements are confirmed.
- AI copy includes human-review paths for risky, regulated, unclear, urgent, or high-value leads.
- Terms explain that AI output may require human review.
- Privacy and Terms pages are linked in the footer.
- Legal docs note that final legal language should be reviewed by an attorney before launch.

## Remaining Manual Deployment Checks

Complete these checks on the live production domain:

- Confirm `NEXT_PUBLIC_SITE_URL` is set to the final production URL in Vercel.
- Visit `/sitemap.xml` and confirm every URL uses the production domain, not `localhost` or a preview URL.
- Visit `/robots.txt` and confirm it references the production sitemap.
- Open core, service, industry, and alternative pages on mobile.
- Submit a real test lead through the audit form and confirm the success state appears.
- Submit a real test lead through the demo form and confirm the intake result appears.
- Check the browser console for hydration or runtime errors.
- Test social sharing previews in LinkedIn, Facebook, X/Twitter, Slack, and messages.
- Run Lighthouse or PageSpeed Insights on the homepage, audit page, and one SEO landing page.
- Confirm analytics events fire only after analytics env vars are configured.
