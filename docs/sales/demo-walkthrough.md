# SignalOps Demo Walkthrough

## Goal Of The Demo

Show skeptical business owners that SignalOps is an operational system, not a chatbot gimmick. Keep the demo practical, concrete, and tied to booking outcomes.

## Demo Flow At A Glance

1. SignalOps homepage  
2. RouteWash Mobile Fleet Care demo  
3. Missed Lead Check form  
4. Dashboard  
5. How-it-works page  
6. Closing pitch

## 1) SignalOps Homepage Walkthrough

Talk track:

"Most businesses do not have a lead volume problem. They have a response and follow-up execution problem. SignalOps closes that gap."

What to point out:
- Core promise: instant response, intake, and booking flow.
- Problem framing: leads get missed after form submit, missed call, DM, or ad inquiry.
- Packages: clear path from starter to custom system.
- CTA: Free Missed Lead Check as low-friction next step.

## 2) RouteWash Mobile Fleet Care Demo Walkthrough

Talk track:

"Here is the same concept inside a real local-service scenario."

Narrative to present step-by-step:

1. Customer submits fleet wash quote request:
   "A fleet manager asks for biweekly washing for 28 service vans across two locations."
2. AI identifies account context:
   "The system parses fleet size, vehicle type, service frequency, and timing from plain language."
3. AI asks for missing account details:
   "If details are missing, it requests locations, wash window, water access, and site notes."
4. AI flags recurring account opportunities:
   "Higher-value recurring requests get a clearer owner handoff."
5. AI prepares quote or callback path:
   "Route-friendly requests are packaged differently than one-time or unclear requests."
6. AI sends internal sales note:
   "Owner/rep gets summary, urgency, and next action without reading a full thread."
7. Dashboard updates automatically:
   "Pipeline, score, tags, and status reflect the new lead immediately."

## 3) Missed Lead Check Form Walkthrough

Talk track:

"This is how we turn interest into diagnosis and implementation scope."

What to point out:
- Form gathers business context, lead volume, tools, and bottlenecks.
- Submission creates a structured intake, not a vague contact request.
- Confirmation includes a practical preview and next steps.

## 4) Dashboard Walkthrough

Talk track:

"This is the owner/operator view after implementation."

Show:
- Priority value, urgency, and recommended action.
- Pipeline movement from new to booked/won/lost.
- Lead detail card with customer message, AI summary, internal note, and tags.
- Timeline events:
  - Lead submitted
  - AI ready
  - Customer reply prepared/sent
  - Owner alert prepared/sent
  - Follow-up sequence started

## 5) How-It-Works Page Walkthrough

Talk track:

"This explains the engine in plain terms for non-technical owners and sales teams."

Highlight:
- Lead capture from forms/calls/ads/DMs.
- Instant response and intake.
- Scoring and routing.
- Booking handoff.
- Follow-up sequence.
- Dashboard visibility.

Use this page when someone says, "Can you explain exactly what your system does?"

## 6) Closing Pitch

Primary close:

"If this makes sense, the next step is a Free Missed Lead Check. We look at how you handle calls, texts, forms, DMs, and follow-up, then show where leads are getting missed, delayed, or forgotten."

Secondary close:

"We can start with a pilot around one lead source and one workflow. You get measurable wins before expanding."

## Common Objections During Demo

### "We already follow up manually."

"That is usually the issue. Manual works until volume rises, then consistency drops. We automate the repetitive layer so your team handles the right conversations faster."

### "Is this replacing my staff?"

"No. It improves staff output by handling response, intake prep, and follow-up consistency."

### "Will this work with my current tools?"

"Yes. SignalOps is designed to integrate with existing forms, CRM, phone, email, SMS, and calendar workflows."
