# SignalOps Discovery Call Script

## Opening

Thanks for making time.

The goal of this call is simple: understand how your leads move from first inquiry to booked appointment, identify where calls, texts, forms, DMs, or follow-ups are being missed, and decide if a SignalOps pilot is worth running.

If there is a fit, I will recommend a practical first implementation. If not, I will still share the top fixes I see.

## Current Lead Flow Questions

- Where do most new leads come from right now?
- What happens in the first 5 minutes after a lead comes in?
- Who owns first response during business hours?
- Who owns first response after hours and weekends?
- What does your current handoff from lead to appointment look like?

## Pain Questions

- Where do leads usually stall?
- What frustrates your team most about lead follow-up?
- Which lead source feels the messiest operationally?
- How often do you lose leads you thought would book?
- What does a "missed opportunity" usually look like in your business?

## Intake Questions

- What makes a lead high quality for your team?
- What signals make a lead low intent or poor fit?
- What info do you need before someone can be booked?
- How do you currently prioritize hot leads versus cold leads?

## Tool and CRM Questions

- What CRM or pipeline tool do you use today?
- Is the CRM actually used daily or mostly treated as a log?
- Which tools handle calls, forms, email, text, and calendar booking?
- Where are leads currently tracked outside your CRM?

## Quote Request Questions

- How are quote requests collected right now?
- Do quote requests arrive with enough context to price quickly?
- How often do quote requests go unanswered or delayed?
- Do you have different quote workflows by service type?

## Photo Submission Questions

- Do you require photos for quoting?
- If photos are missing, what process asks for them today?
- How many quote requests die before photos are collected?
- For auto/wheel businesses: how do you separate cosmetic from safety-sensitive damage?

## Budget and Value Questions

- What is an average booked job value?
- What is one additional booked job per week worth?
- If response speed improved, what business outcome matters most?
- What would make a pilot an obvious win in the first 30-45 days?

## Objection Handling

### "We already have a CRM."

Great. SignalOps works with existing CRMs. We are not replacing your system. We are fixing response, intake, and follow-up execution around it.

### "We need more leads, not automation."

Totally fair. In many businesses, the fastest growth lift comes from converting more of the leads you already paid for before scaling ad spend.

### "AI sounds risky for customer conversations."

Agreed. The system should be conservative and operational, not gimmicky. High-risk or uncertain scenarios are escalated to a human by design.

### "My team can just follow up manually."

They can, but manual consistency is the bottleneck at scale. The point is to remove repetitive follow-up load so your team can focus on real conversations and closing.

### "Not sure this is a priority right now."

Understood. If helpful, we can do a lightweight audit first and quantify where leads are being missed. Then you can decide based on clear numbers, not guesswork.
