# SignalOps Prospecting System

## Purpose

This is a practical outbound system to help SignalOps find, contact, and close first pilot clients.

Use this as a daily operating plan, not a theory document.

## 1) Best First Niches

Start with niches where inbound quote flow exists, response speed matters, and owners are too busy to run tight follow-up manually.

1. Wheel repair shops
2. Auto repair shops
3. Detailers

9. HVAC

14. Real estate teams

## 2) Why Wheel Repair and Auto Service Is a Strong First Niche

Wheel repair and adjacent auto service is a high-leverage first wedge for SignalOps:

- Quote requests are common and frequent.
- Customers often send photos, which makes AI-assisted intake useful immediately.
- Fast response matters because customers are comparison shopping and often want same-week service.
- Missed calls are common during shop hours.
- Jobs can be high-value, especially multi-wheel refinishing and powder coating.
- Owners are usually busy in the shop, not in the inbox.
- Many shops have weak lead handling after form submit.
- Mobile service scheduling is often messy without routing logic.

## 3) Prospect Qualification Criteria

Use this checklist before outreach. A prospect is qualified when at least 6 of 9 are true.

- Has a website
- Has a contact form or quote request flow
- Has phone number displayed prominently
- Has active Instagram or Facebook presence
- Shows before/after work
- Offers estimates or quote requests
- Lead capture looks weak or slow
- Average customer value appears high enough to support a pilot
- Owner or operator appears reachable

Quick scoring model:

- 2 points: strong signal
- 1 point: partial signal
- 0 points: missing signal
- Outreach now: 12+ points
- Warm later: 8 to 11 points
- Skip: below 8 points

## 4) Manual Research Process

Run this sequence in order so your list quality stays high.

1. Google Maps search:
   Use terms like `wheel repair dallas`, `rim repair near me`, `auto detail [city]`, `tint shop [city]`.
2. Open each website:
   Check lead flow, form quality, CTA clarity, and booking friction.
3. Open Instagram and Facebook:
   Confirm recent activity, owner tone, and service focus.
4. Check local directories:
   Yelp, BBB, local service directories, niche listings.
5. Check local communities:
   Car groups, enthusiast pages, neighborhood groups, chamber listings.
6. Use your network:
   Ask for intros to owner-operators in auto service and home service.

Research output per prospect:

- One observed missed lead sign
- One likely business impact
- One clear offer angle for first message

## 5) Missed Lead Signs To Look For

Use these as trigger conditions for outreach:

- Generic contact forms with no intake structure
- No photo upload or photo request flow
- No instant quote response expectation
- No mobile service CTA where mobile work is relevant
- No missed-call text back system
- Slow-looking email handling
- No follow-up offer if customer does not reply
- Weak mobile CTA visibility
- No booking flow
- No clear service area

## 6) Outreach Tracker Fields

Track every prospect in one sheet or CRM with these fields:

- Business name
- Owner/contact
- Website
- Niche
- Missed lead observed
- Outreach channel
- Message sent
- Follow-up date
- Status
- Notes

Suggested `status` values:

- New
- Researched
- First touch sent
- Follow-up 1
- Follow-up 2
- Replied
- Audit booked
- No response
- Not fit
- Won pilot

## 7) Daily Execution Plan

Daily targets:

- 20 DMs
- 10 emails
- 5 warm asks

Weekly targets:

- 3 Loom audits per week
- 5 booked discovery calls per week
- 2 pilot proposals sent per week

Daily schedule example:

1. 60 minutes research and list build
2. 60 minutes DMs and first-touch emails
3. 30 minutes follow-ups
4. 30 minutes warm intro asks
5. 30 minutes tracker cleanup and next-day prep

Non-negotiables:

- No day ends with unlogged outreach.
- Follow-up dates are always set at send time.
- Every message references one real missed lead observation.

## 9) Pilot Close Script

Use this once they agree there is a real lead handling gap.

`Based on what you shared, the issue is not demand. The issue is response speed, qualification structure, and follow-up consistency.`

`The pilot is designed to fix that without overhauling your whole business.`

`In 30 days, we install a focused AI Lead Response and Qualification System for your highest-value lead path.`

`That includes instant first response, qualification prompts, priority scoring, routing, and follow-up until booking or disqualification.`

`Pilot pricing is [insert current pilot pricing].`

`If this performs, we expand. If not, you still leave with a cleaner process and clearer data.`

`Do you want to move forward with a pilot scope for [Business Name]?`

## Weekly Review Loop

Run this every Friday:

1. Count messages sent by channel
2. Count replies by niche
3. Count audits booked
4. Count proposals sent
5. Count pilots closed
6. Identify top-performing message by niche
7. Rewrite weakest message before next Monday

This keeps the system repeatable and improves close rate every week.
