# SignalOps Loom Demo Script

Purpose: record a tight 3-minute Loom that shows a prospect how SignalOps captures, routes, and follows up with leads using the SignalOps homepage, RouteWash Mobile Fleet Care demo, and dashboard.

Tone: conversational, confident, practical, and not pushy.

## Recording Setup

Open these tabs before recording:

1. SignalOps homepage: `/`
2. RouteWash Mobile Fleet Care demo: `/demo#lead-form`
3. SignalOps dashboard: `/dashboard`
4. Free Missed Lead Check: `/audit`

Use this main scenario in the demo form:

- Name: Avery Stone
- Phone: (214) 555-0149
- Email: avery@example.com
- Fleet: 28 HVAC service vans
- Locations: Dallas + Irving
- Service needed: Recurring wash plan
- Fleet size: 28
- Water access: Yes
- After-hours service: Yes, preferred
- Site notes: Water access is available at both locations. Current provider is inconsistent on timing.
- Description: Need biweekly washing for service vans without slowing down daily routes.
- Preferred wash window: After-hours on weeknights

Simple recording rule: move quickly, show the actual forms/results, and talk about revenue protection instead of "AI magic."

## Primary 3-Minute Script

### 0:00-0:15 - Quick Intro

Screen action: Start on the SignalOps homepage hero.

Say:

"Hey, this is a quick walkthrough of SignalOps. The idea is simple: when someone fills out a form, calls, clicks an ad, or asks for a quote, the business should not have to wait hours to respond, collect details the lead, or figure out who should follow up. SignalOps installs that response system."

Timing note: Keep this under 15 seconds. Do not explain the whole product yet.

### 0:15-0:35 - Identify the Missed Lead Problem

Screen action: Scroll just enough to show the problem or solution section on the homepage.

Say:

"Most local businesses do not have a lead problem first. They have a response, intake, and follow-up problem. A good lead comes in, but nobody responds fast enough. Or the team responds, but they do not know if the lead is urgent, ready, worth a quote, missing details, or should be booked right away."

Timing note: This is the pain. Say it plainly and move on.

### 0:35-0:55 - Show the SignalOps Homepage

Screen action: Point to the homepage CTA and core offer language.

Say:

"SignalOps builds an AI Lead Response and Intake System around that process. It can respond instantly, ask the right questions, sort priority, alert the owner or sales rep, log the lead, and start follow-up if the customer does not book."

Screen action: Click or point to the "View Demo" link, then move to the RouteWash Mobile Fleet Care demo tab.

### 0:55-1:25 - Show the RouteWash Mobile Fleet Care Demo Form

Screen action: On `/demo`, scroll to the lead capture form.

Say:

"Here is a realistic example for RouteWash Mobile Fleet Care, a mobile fleet wash company in Dallas-Fort Worth. A fleet manager has 28 service vans across two locations and wants a biweekly after-hours wash quote."

Screen action: Show the form fields as you speak. If the form is prefilled, briefly point at the important values. If not, enter them quickly from the setup list.

Say:

"Instead of a generic contact form that just says 'we will get back to you,' this captures the details the owner actually needs: fleet size, vehicle types, location count, service area, frequency, wash window, water access, and site notes."

Timing note: Do not read every field. The viewer can see the fields.

### 1:25-1:40 - Submit the Realistic Lead

Screen action: Click the form submit button.

Say:

"Now I am going to submit this like a real quote request. In production, this would trigger a text or email reply, owner alert, CRM log, and follow-up sequence."

Timing note: Pause half a second after clicking so the result card can load.

### 1:40-2:10 - Show the AI Intake Result

Screen action: Focus on the result card. Point to score, priority, summary, recommended action, suggested reply, internal note, and tags.

Say:

"Here is the important part. The system does not just store the lead. It packages it. This one is a recurring account opportunity: 28 vans, two locations, after-hours preference, and biweekly cadence. The owner gets priority, summary, recommended next action, suggested customer reply, and internal note."

Say:

"For the customer, the reply can acknowledge the request, ask for missing site details if needed, and move them toward a quote or callback. For the owner, the note explains what to do next without making the team reread the whole message."

Timing note: Use the result on screen. If the result score varies, do not quote the exact number unless it is visible.

### 2:10-2:35 - Show the Dashboard

Screen action: Move to `/dashboard`.

Say:

"Then the dashboard gives the owner or manager a working view of the lead pipeline. They can see hot leads, account detail requests, booked jobs, missed opportunities, and which leads need a response."

Screen action: Click or point to a lead detail card if available.

Say:

"For each lead, they can see the contact info, fleet details, request type, AI summary, suggested reply, internal sales note, tags, and timeline. This is what makes it useful day to day. It is not just another inbox."

### 2:35-2:50 - Connect It to Their Business

Screen action: Stay on the dashboard or briefly return to the homepage.

Say:

"For your business, the same system can connect to your website forms, missed calls, ads, DMs, email inquiries, quote requests, detail submissions, CRM, and booking calendar. The exact questions and routing rules change by industry, but the goal is the same: respond faster, collect better details, and follow up consistently."

Timing note: Make this about their operation, not the demo.

### 2:50-3:00 - Close With Missed Lead Check CTA

Screen action: Open or point to `/audit`.

Say:

"If you want, I can run a Free Missed Lead Check and show where calls, texts, forms, DMs, and follow-ups are getting missed, delayed, or forgotten. It is not a sales pitch first. It is a practical look at the way you already handle leads, with or without a CRM."

Timing note: End cleanly. Do not over-explain after the CTA.

## Alternate Demo Scenarios

### Large Fleet With Multiple Locations

Use when you want to show owner routing.

Example form changes:

- Fleet size: 65
- Number of locations: 4
- After-hours service: Yes
- Description: Need monthly washing across several lots and want to know if route pricing is available.
- Site notes: Needs access details confirmed.

Say:

"This is a higher-value account lead because the customer mentions multiple locations and route pricing. The system should avoid promising final pricing, alert the owner, and collect the details needed for a responsible quote."

Value point:

"SignalOps can separate a casual quote request from something that needs immediate human attention."

### Site Requirements Need Confirmation

Use when you want to show conservative handling.

Example form changes:

- Water access: Unsure
- Description: We need dealership lot washing, but I am not sure what site setup is required.
- Site notes: Need to confirm parking, access, and local site requirements.

Say:

"Site requirements should be handled carefully. The system can collect access and timing details, but it should not promise compliance or service setup before RouteWash confirms the local requirements."

Value point:

"Good automation should protect the business, not create risky claims."

### Dealership Lot Refresh

Use when you want to show high-value opportunity sorting.

Example form changes:

- Service needed: Dealership lot refresh
- Fleet size: 80
- After-hours service: Yes
- Description: Need inventory cleaned before weekend sales traffic.
- Site notes: Lot access available after 7pm.

Say:

"This is not an emergency, but it can be a higher-value opportunity because many vehicles and a recurring relationship may be involved. The system can tag it as dealership inventory, collect site notes, and push the customer toward an account quote."

Value point:

"Priority sorting is not only about emergencies. It also helps identify revenue value."

### Customer Missing Site Details

Use when you want to show follow-up automation.

Example form changes:

- Fleet size: 18
- Service needed: Service van washing
- Site notes: Not sure about water access yet.
- Description: Need a quote for vans, but not sure which wash window works.

Say:

"This lead is interested, but the owner probably needs site details before quoting. The system can instantly reply with the right questions, tag the lead as details pending, and follow up if the customer does not respond."

Value point:

"A lot of revenue is lost in the gap between 'send the details' and the customer actually sending them. That follow-up can be automated."

## Industry-Specific Versions

### Mobile Fleet Wash Companies

Use this opener:

"I built this around a mobile fleet wash example because your leads are usually quote-based, account-based, and timing-sensitive. A fleet manager may need service vans washed, dealership inventory refreshed, or recurring service across multiple locations, and each one needs a different response."

Use this value statement:

"SignalOps helps sort recurring account opportunities from one-time requests, asks for site details when needed, routes priority leads to the owner, and follows up until the customer books or clearly drops off."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at your quote form, fleet detail intake, route scheduling CTA, missed-call process, texts, DMs, and follow-up after estimates."

### Auto Repair Shops

Use this opener:

"For an auto shop, the same system works around service inquiries, missed calls, appointment requests, and symptoms that may need priority handling."

Use this value statement:

"If someone says the car is shaking, leaking, will not start, or needs same-day help, the system can flag urgency and alert the team. If it is routine maintenance, it can collect vehicle info and move them toward booking."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at how fast you respond to service requests, whether missed calls get texted back, and how many quote or appointment inquiries never get followed up."

### Detailers

Use this opener:

"For a detailing business, missed leads usually come from package confusion, slow follow-up, and not collecting enough information to quote properly."

Use this value statement:

"SignalOps can ask about vehicle type, condition, desired package, location, photos, timing, and whether they want mobile or shop service. Then it can suggest the next best reply and push the customer toward booking."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at your package flow, photo requests, mobile scheduling, quote follow-up, and review or rebooking opportunities."

### Med Spas

Use this opener:

"For a med spa, the system is not about replacing your team. It is about responding quickly, collecting the right inquiry details, and routing consult requests appropriately."

Use this value statement:

"SignalOps can capture treatment interest, timeline, location, preferred contact method, and booking intent. Anything sensitive or uncertain can be flagged for human review."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at consult requests, missed calls, slow follow-up, booking drop-off, and where human review should stay in the process."

### Insurance Agencies

Use this opener:

"For an insurance agency, speed matters because quote shoppers often contact several agencies at once."

Use this value statement:

"SignalOps can respond immediately, collect the line of insurance, timing, current coverage status, contact details, and route the lead to the right producer. It can also follow up when a quote shopper goes quiet."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at inbound quote forms, call handling, producer handoff, CRM logging if you use one, and follow-up on unbound quotes."
