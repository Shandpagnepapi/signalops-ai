# SignalOps Loom Demo Script

Purpose: record a tight 3-minute Loom that shows a prospect how SignalOps captures, qualifies, routes, and follows up with leads using the SignalOps homepage, Apex Wheel Repair demo, and dashboard.

Tone: conversational, confident, practical, and not pushy.

## Recording Setup

Open these tabs before recording:

1. SignalOps homepage: `/`
2. Apex Wheel Repair demo: `/demo#lead-form`
3. SignalOps dashboard: `/dashboard`
4. Free Lead Leak Audit: `/audit`

Use this main scenario in the demo form:

- Name: Avery Stone
- Phone: (214) 555-0149
- Email: avery@example.com
- Vehicle: 2022 Tesla Model Y
- Wheel size: 20 inch
- Damage type: Curb rash / scuffed lip
- Number of wheels damaged: 2
- Vehicle drivable: Yes
- Mobile service: Yes, if possible
- Photo notes: Photos show curb rash on the front passenger wheel and chipped paint on the rear wheel.
- Description: Looking for a quote and mobile appointment this week if possible.
- Preferred appointment time: Tomorrow afternoon

Simple recording rule: move quickly, show the actual forms/results, and talk about revenue protection instead of "AI magic."

## Primary 3-Minute Script

### 0:00-0:15 - Quick Intro

Screen action: Start on the SignalOps homepage hero.

Say:

"Hey, this is a quick walkthrough of SignalOps. The idea is simple: when someone fills out a form, calls, clicks an ad, or asks for a quote, the business should not have to wait hours to respond, qualify the lead, or figure out who should follow up. SignalOps installs that response system."

Timing note: Keep this under 15 seconds. Do not explain the whole product yet.

### 0:15-0:35 - Identify the Missed Lead Problem

Screen action: Scroll just enough to show the problem or solution section on the homepage.

Say:

"Most local businesses do not have a lead problem first. They have a response, qualification, and follow-up problem. A good lead comes in, but nobody responds fast enough. Or the team responds, but they do not know if the lead is urgent, qualified, worth a quote, needs photos, or should be booked right away."

Timing note: This is the pain. Say it plainly and move on.

### 0:35-0:55 - Show the SignalOps Homepage

Screen action: Point to the homepage CTA and core offer language.

Say:

"SignalOps builds an AI Lead Response and Qualification System around that process. It can respond instantly, ask the right questions, score the opportunity, alert the owner or sales rep, log the lead, and start follow-up if the customer does not book."

Screen action: Click or point to the "View Demo" link, then move to the Apex Wheel Repair demo tab.

### 0:55-1:25 - Show the Apex Wheel Repair Demo Form

Screen action: On `/demo`, scroll to the lead capture form.

Say:

"Here is a realistic example for Apex Wheel Repair, a wheel repair and refinishing shop in Dallas. A customer has curb rash on two wheels, wants a quote, asks if mobile repair is available, and can send photos."

Screen action: Show the form fields as you speak. If the form is prefilled, briefly point at the important values. If not, enter them quickly from the setup list.

Say:

"Instead of a generic contact form that just says 'we will get back to you,' this captures the details the shop actually needs: vehicle, wheel size, damage type, number of wheels, whether the car is drivable, whether mobile service is requested, photo notes, and timing."

Timing note: Do not read every field. The viewer can see the fields.

### 1:25-1:40 - Submit the Realistic Lead

Screen action: Click the form submit button.

Say:

"Now I am going to submit this like a real quote request. In production, this would trigger a text or email reply, owner alert, CRM log, and follow-up sequence."

Timing note: Pause half a second after clicking so the result card can load.

### 1:40-2:10 - Show the AI Qualification Result

Screen action: Focus on the result card. Point to score, priority, summary, recommended action, suggested reply, internal note, and tags.

Say:

"Here is the important part. The system does not just store the lead. It qualifies it. This one is a solid cosmetic repair lead: two wheels, mobile service requested, customer can send photos, and they want an appointment this week. The shop gets a score, priority, summary, recommended next action, suggested customer reply, and internal note."

Say:

"For the customer, the reply can acknowledge the issue, ask for photos if needed, and move them toward a quote or appointment. For the shop, the note explains what to do next without making the team reread the whole message."

Timing note: Use the result on screen. If the result score varies, do not quote the exact number unless it is visible.

### 2:10-2:35 - Show the Dashboard

Screen action: Move to `/dashboard`.

Say:

"Then the dashboard gives the owner or manager a working view of the lead pipeline. They can see hot leads, photo requests, booked appointments, missed opportunities, and which leads need a response."

Screen action: Click or point to a lead detail card if available.

Say:

"For each lead, they can see the contact info, vehicle details, damage type, AI summary, suggested reply, internal sales note, tags, and timeline. This is what makes it useful day to day. It is not just another inbox."

### 2:35-2:50 - Connect It to Their Business

Screen action: Stay on the dashboard or briefly return to the homepage.

Say:

"For your business, the same system can connect to your website forms, missed calls, ads, DMs, email inquiries, quote requests, photo submissions, CRM, and booking calendar. The exact questions and routing rules change by industry, but the goal is the same: respond faster, qualify better, and follow up consistently."

Timing note: Make this about their operation, not the demo.

### 2:50-3:00 - Close With Lead Leak Audit CTA

Screen action: Open or point to `/audit`.

Say:

"If you want, I can run a Free Lead Leak Audit and show where calls, texts, forms, DMs, and follow-ups are getting missed, delayed, or forgotten. It is not a sales pitch first. It is a practical look at the way you already handle leads, with or without a CRM."

Timing note: End cleanly. Do not over-explain after the CTA.

## Alternate Demo Scenarios

### Bent Wheel and Vehicle Shaking

Use when you want to show urgent routing.

Example form changes:

- Damage type: Bent wheel
- Number of wheels damaged: 1
- Vehicle drivable: No or unsure
- Mobile service: No
- Description: Hit a pothole and now the car shakes at highway speed. Need to know if it is safe to drive and whether it can be fixed.
- Photo notes: No photos yet.

Say:

"This is a higher urgency lead because the customer mentions a bent wheel and shaking at highway speed. The system should not make unsafe promises. It should flag this for shop inspection, alert the team quickly, and guide the customer toward the safest next step."

Value point:

"SignalOps can separate a casual quote request from something that needs immediate human attention."

### Cracked Wheel Needing Inspection

Use when you want to show conservative AI handling.

Example form changes:

- Damage type: Cracked wheel
- Number of wheels damaged: 1
- Vehicle drivable: No or unsure
- Description: Wheel is leaking air and I can see a crack. Can this be welded or do I need a replacement?
- Photo notes: Can send close-up photos.

Say:

"A cracked wheel should be handled carefully. The system can collect photos and details, but it should recommend inspection and human review instead of promising a repair. If the wheel is structurally unsafe, the shop can recommend replacement."

Value point:

"Good automation should protect the business, not create risky claims."

### Full Set of Wheels Needing Refinishing

Use when you want to show high-value opportunity scoring.

Example form changes:

- Damage type: Refinishing or powder coating
- Number of wheels damaged: 4
- Vehicle drivable: Yes
- Mobile service: No
- Description: I want all four OEM wheels refinished black. Looking for pricing and appointment availability.
- Photo notes: Photos available.

Say:

"This is not urgent in the safety sense, but it can be a higher-value opportunity because all four wheels are involved. The system can tag it as refinishing, collect photos, ask finish preferences, and push the customer toward a scheduled estimate."

Value point:

"Lead scoring is not only about emergencies. It also helps identify revenue value."

### Customer Missing Photos

Use when you want to show follow-up automation.

Example form changes:

- Damage type: Curb rash
- Number of wheels damaged: 1 or 2
- Vehicle drivable: Yes
- Mobile service: Yes
- Photo notes: Not sure how to upload photos yet.
- Description: Need a quote for scuffed wheels.

Say:

"This lead is interested, but the shop probably needs photos before quoting. The system can instantly reply with photo instructions, tag the lead as photo request pending, and follow up if the customer does not send them."

Value point:

"A lot of revenue is lost in the gap between 'send photos' and the customer actually sending them. That follow-up can be automated."

## Industry-Specific Versions

### Wheel Repair Shops

Use this opener:

"I built this around a wheel repair example because your leads are usually quote-based, photo-based, and time-sensitive. A customer may have curb rash, a bent wheel, a cracked wheel, or refinishing work, and each one needs a different response."

Use this value statement:

"SignalOps helps sort cosmetic mobile opportunities from inspection-required work, asks for photos when needed, routes urgent leads to the shop, and follows up until the customer books or clearly drops off."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at your quote form, photo intake, mobile repair CTA, missed-call process, texts, DMs, and follow-up after estimates."

### Auto Repair Shops

Use this opener:

"For an auto shop, the same system works around service inquiries, missed calls, appointment requests, and symptoms that may need priority handling."

Use this value statement:

"If someone says the car is shaking, leaking, will not start, or needs same-day help, the system can flag urgency and alert the team. If it is routine maintenance, it can collect vehicle info and move them toward booking."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at how fast you respond to service requests, whether missed calls get texted back, and how many quote or appointment inquiries never get followed up."

### Detailers

Use this opener:

"For a detailing business, missed leads usually come from package confusion, slow follow-up, and not collecting enough information to quote properly."

Use this value statement:

"SignalOps can ask about vehicle type, condition, desired package, location, photos, timing, and whether they want mobile or shop service. Then it can suggest the next best reply and push the customer toward booking."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at your package flow, photo requests, mobile scheduling, quote follow-up, and review or rebooking opportunities."

### Med Spas

Use this opener:

"For a med spa, the system is not about replacing your team. It is about responding quickly, collecting the right inquiry details, and routing consult requests appropriately."

Use this value statement:

"SignalOps can capture treatment interest, timeline, location, preferred contact method, and booking intent. Anything sensitive or uncertain can be flagged for human review."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at consult requests, missed calls, slow follow-up, booking drop-off, and where human review should stay in the process."

### Insurance Agencies

Use this opener:

"For an insurance agency, speed matters because quote shoppers often contact several agencies at once."

Use this value statement:

"SignalOps can respond immediately, collect the line of insurance, timing, current coverage status, contact details, and route the lead to the right producer. It can also follow up when a quote shopper goes quiet."

Use this close:

"The audit would look at inbound quote forms, call handling, producer handoff, CRM logging if you use one, and follow-up on unbound quotes."
